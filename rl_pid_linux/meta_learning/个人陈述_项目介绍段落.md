# 个人陈述 - 论文项目介绍段落

## 英文版本（适合申请国外项目）

In my research, I addressed a fundamental challenge in robotic control: automating PID parameter tuning across diverse robotic platforms while maintaining practical sample efficiency for real-world deployment. Despite PID controllers being the dominant choice in over 90% of industrial control loops, manual tuning remains time-consuming and requires extensive domain expertise, limiting scalability across heterogeneous robot morphologies. To solve this problem, I developed a novel hierarchical control framework that synergistically combines meta-learning for cross-platform PID initialization and reinforcement learning (RL) for online adaptation to model uncertainties and disturbances. A key innovation of my work is a physics-based data augmentation strategy that generates 232 high-quality virtual robot configurations from only 3 base platforms by systematically perturbing physical parameters (mass, inertia, friction, damping) within physically realistic bounds, enabling effective meta-learning with minimal real robot data. I validated the approach on two morphologically distinct platforms—a 9-DOF Franka Panda manipulator and a 12-DOF Laikago quadruped robot—achieving a 16.6% average improvement in joint tracking accuracy on Franka Panda (reducing mean absolute error from 7.51° to 6.26°) with exceptional gains in high-load joints (80.4% improvement in shoulder pitch joint). Critically, my research discovered the "optimization ceiling effect": RL achieves dramatic improvements when meta-learning exhibits localized high-error joints, but provides minimal benefit when baseline performance is uniformly strong—a finding with important implications for hierarchical control system design. The method demonstrates robust performance under disturbances (19.2% improvement under parameter uncertainty) with only 10 minutes of training time, validated through comprehensive multi-seed analysis across 100 random initializations. This work, submitted to Robotics and Autonomous Systems, establishes that strategic data curation and hierarchical learning architectures can enable practical, scalable PID tuning across diverse robotic systems, with the complete codebase open-sourced to facilitate reproducibility and future research.

## 中文版本（适合申请国内项目）

在我的研究中，我解决了一个机器人控制领域的核心挑战：如何在保持实际部署所需样本效率的前提下，实现跨多样化机器人平台的PID参数自动调优。尽管PID控制器在超过90%的工业控制回路中占主导地位，但手动调参仍然耗时且需要大量专业知识，限制了方法在不同形态机器人间的可扩展性。为解决这一问题，我开发了一个新颖的分层控制框架，该框架协同结合了用于跨平台PID初始化的元学习和用于在线适应模型不确定性与扰动的强化学习。我工作的一个关键创新是物理基础的数据增强策略，通过系统性地在物理合理范围内扰动物理参数（质量、惯性、摩擦、阻尼），从仅3个基础平台生成了232个高质量的虚拟机器人配置，从而在最小化真实机器人数据需求的情况下实现有效的元学习。我在两个形态迥异的平台上验证了该方法——9自由度Franka Panda机械臂和12自由度Laikago四足机器人——在Franka Panda上实现了16.6%的平均关节跟踪精度提升（平均绝对误差从7.51°降至6.26°），并在高负载关节上取得了显著改进（肩部俯仰关节80.4%的改进）。重要的是，我的研究发现了"优化天花板效应"：当元学习存在局部高误差关节时，强化学习能实现显著改进，但当基线性能均匀强时，强化学习几乎无收益——这一发现对分层控制系统设计具有重要指导意义。该方法在扰动条件下表现出鲁棒性能（参数不确定性下19.2%的改进），仅需10分钟训练时间，并通过100个随机初始化的全面多种子分析得到验证。这项工作已投稿至Robotics and Autonomous Systems期刊，确立了战略性数据管理和分层学习架构能够实现跨多样化机器人系统的实用、可扩展PID调优，完整代码库已开源以促进可重复性和未来研究。
